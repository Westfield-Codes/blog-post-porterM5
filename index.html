Cheese Rolling: The Worlds Most Interesting Sport

The concept is easy: Participants in men's and women's categories chase a circular Double Gloucester cheese down Cooper's Hill, near Brockworth.

I have been obsessed with cheese ever since I got hooked on cheese whiz. I've since moved on to stronger cheeses such as colby jack and blue cheese. That’s why I was fascinated to learn about my new favorite sport: Cheese Rolling.

The historical event has been taking place in England annually since 1826. Competitors attempt to race a 8 pound cheese rolling down a hill, whoever beats the cheese, wins the race.

This event often leads to injuries due to the danger of sprinting down a hill at full speed. The cheese moves so fast that is has the potential to knock over participants. There are different events for men and woman, all taking place on the same day.

What Is Competitive Shin-Kicking?

This brutal sport involves my favorite activity, hitting my shins. I often use a scooter, swing, or even a baseball bat to apply agonizing pain to my tibia.


Shin-Kicking is a sport that involves two contestants attempting to kick each other on the shin in order to force their opponent to the ground. It originated in early 17th century England.

It was one of the most popular events at the Cotswold Olimpick Games. Today, it remains one of the popular events at the games.

Opponents attempt to kick the other persons shin, winning when the contestant that gives up says “sufficient”.
